# Commissioner Public Interaction Management System (CPIMS)
# Antigravity Governance Document v1.0
## Demo-First, Citizen-Centric, Reliable Execution Charter

---

### 1. Purpose of This Document
This document establishes the governance model for the continued design, build, validation, and evolution of the Commissioner Public Interaction Management System (CPIMS) using Antigravity as a disciplined multi-agent execution control plane.

It is intended to ensure that all future work proceeds with:
* clear business-rule fidelity
* strict ownership of product truth
* parallel but controlled execution
* artifact-driven validation
* high demo reliability
* scalable product maturity

This is not a generic development brief. It is the operating charter for how CPIMS will be built, extended, and governed.

---

### 2. Mission
Build a polished, reliable, citizen-centric digital service platform for Commissioner office public interaction management.

CPIMS shall not be treated as a generic appointment application. It is a public interaction operating system prototype that must convincingly demonstrate:
* clear public access rules
* guided citizen intake
* preservation of walk-in access where applicable
* disciplined staff triage and scheduling
* transparent citizen tracking
* executive visibility
* credible AI-ready capability surfaces

The immediate target is a high-clarity, high-discipline, commissioner-impressing working prototype.

---

### 3. Strategic Product Intent
CPIMS is intended to function as a digital public interaction and service governance platform for Commissioner office operations.

It must not be reduced to any one of the following:
* a simple appointment portal
* a grievance form
* a static schedule display
* a dashboard-only solution

It is to be developed as a unified operating layer for:
* public access governance
* citizen request intake
* walk-in support
* appointment-based engagement
* project review participation
* staff operations workflow
* executive visibility
* future AI-assisted service intelligence

---

### 4. Primary Build Objective
Deliver a working digital prototype that demonstrates the following complete outcome:

A citizen can understand today’s public access rule, choose the correct interaction path, submit a request where needed, track progress transparently, and receive timely updates, while staff can triage and act through a structured workflow and leadership can view a real-time operational summary.

The prototype must feel:
* modern
* administrative
* trustworthy
* future-ready
* citizen-friendly
* operationally disciplined

---

### 5. Core Operating Principle
All work on CPIMS shall follow one foundational rule:
**Every feature must be governed by locked shared truth before execution begins.**

Antigravity shall not be used as an unconstrained coding assistant. It shall be used as a multi-agent execution control plane for disciplined, parallel, verifiable delivery.

This means:
* no feature proceeds without business-truth definition
* no lane is permitted to redefine shared rules
* no metrics are allowed without definitional clarity
* no demo surface is accepted without browser/runtime validation
* no “AI feature” is introduced without clear advisory boundaries

---

### 6. Governance Principles
**6.1 Citizen First:** The platform must reduce uncertainty, repeated visits, and procedural confusion for citizens.
**6.2 Administrative Discipline:** The platform must support a controlled and credible office workflow for intake, review, scheduling, and closure.
**6.3 Preserve Existing Public Practice:** Digital systems must clarify and strengthen current public-access norms, not replace them with avoidable barriers.
**6.4 Transparency with Dignity:** Citizens must be able to see meaningful status and updates without exposure to internal-only administrative data.
**6.5 AI as Assistance, Not Authority:** AI capabilities may assist with intake, summarization, prioritization, and communication, but final authority remains with office staff.
**6.6 Artifact-Driven Reliability:** Every meaningful execution step must produce inspectable artifacts, not just code output.
**6.7 Demo-First Practicality:** The immediate goal is a world-class demonstration of service model quality, not premature production infrastructure.

---

### 7. Antigravity Execution Model
Antigravity shall execute CPIMS through five controlled execution lanes.

**Lane A — Business Truth and Product Contract**
*Mandate: Protect truth.* (Source of truth. No feature lane may violate its outputs.)
* Responsibilities: business-rule fidelity, lifecycle states, day-wise access policy, definitions of walk-in/appointment/project review flows, wording standards, information boundaries, metric definitions, notification truth, AI surface truth.

**Lane B — Citizen Experience**
*Mandate: Make the citizen experience unmistakably clear.*
* Responsibilities: homepage, rule clarity, schedule presentation, guidance flow, submission, tracking, walk-in path, public-safe communication visibility.

**Lane C — Staff Operations**
*Mandate: Make office handling look disciplined and administratively credible.*
* Responsibilities: request review, lifecycle actions, scheduling, public/internal notes, transition enforcement, communication intent actions, AI-assisted surfaces.

**Lane D — Executive Visibility**
*Mandate: Make leadership feel immediate control and visibility.*
* Responsibilities: dashboard, KPI summaries, day mode, category distribution, queue/load visibility, communication KPIs, AI usage/assistance summaries.

**Lane E — Verification and Demo Readiness**
*Mandate: Validate reality through runtime and browser-based proof.*
* Responsibilities: browser walkthrough validation, screenshots, mismatch identification, wording validation, metric-truth checks, demo-path integrity, phase exit verification.

---

### 8. Shared Truth Registry
The truth domains are maintained centrally (in `TRUTH_REGISTRY.md`) under Lane A ownership: Schedule Truth, Lifecycle Truth, Public/Internal Boundary, Metric Truth, Notification Truth, and AI Surface Truth. *No phase may proceed without referencing and extending this registry.*

---

### 9. Non-Negotiable Business Truths
* **Monday / Tuesday / Friday**: Walk-in public interaction is allowed. Pre-registration optional; no approval required.
* **Wednesday**: Project review participation. Controlled submission required.
* **Thursday**: Appointment-based open house. Prior submission and staff-assigned slot required.
* **Saturday**: Inspections/non-public functions. No general public access.

*The system must preserve existing public access practices.*

---

### 10. Lifecycle Contract
* **States:** submitted, under_review, approved, rescheduled, rejected, attended, closed.
* **Transitions:** submitted → under_review; under_review → approved | rescheduled | rejected; approved → attended | closed; rescheduled → approved | rejected; attended → closed.
* **Fields:** rejectionReason, rescheduleReason, publicOfficeNote, internalStaffNote.
* **Boundaries:** internalStaffNote remains private. citizen tracking shows only public-safe info.

---

### 11. Metric Contract
No KPI may be introduced without explicit definition. All metrics must be derivable from shared record truth, consistent, explainable, and validated in Lane E. No fake analytics.

---

### 12. AI Surface Contract
All AI outputs are advisory unless explicitly approved exactly otherwise.
* AI must improve: accessibility, summarization, classification, prioritization, communication assistance.
* AI must not replace: administrative judgment, final classification, final scheduling decision, final citizen-facing decision status.

---

### 13. Communication State Model
Until real gateways integrate, communication is workflow truth.
* **Channels:** email, SMS, WhatsApp, portal-only
* **States:** queued, prepared, sent, failed, skipped
* *Must not falsely imply real gateway delivery unless integrated and verified.*

### 15. Definition of Done for Phase 1
Phase 1 is complete when a citizen can land on the homepage, understand today's access mode, submit a request, receive tracking info, see staff review, and watch the dashboard update—all driven by seeded shared truth.

---

### 16. Design Direction
Modern public administration software.
* **Tone:** light theme, white/soft gray, high readability, restrained blue accents, minimal clutter.
* **Avoid:** dark mode, flashy gradients, glassmorphism, startup excess.
* **Impression:** trust, clarity, seriousness, readiness, citizen respect.

---

### 17. Seeded Scenario Requirements
Prototype must include believable records powering all flows: submitted grievance, under-review project request, approved Thursday slot, rescheduled case, rejected case with public reason, attended/closed case, walk-in pre-registration case.

---

### 18. Artifact Requirements
* **Per Lane:** what was built, assumptions, screenshots, acceptance criteria proof, limitations, mismatches, unresolved decisions.
* **Lane E:** golden walkthrough proof, broken-path report, wording review, dashboard-truth validation, phase exit recommendation.

---

### 19. What Antigravity Must Not Do
* change business rules
* invent statuses
* merge flows improperly
* expose internal notes
* add real third-party integrations early
* overbuild backend complexity
* prioritize novelty over demo clarity
* introduce ungrouped AI or fake metrics

---

### 20. Three-Pass Methodology for All New Features
1. **Pass 1 — Foundation Truth** (Lock business truth, models, schemas. Output: Contracts).
2. **Pass 2 — Parallel Lane Build** (Citizen, Staff, Dashboard consume truth concurrently).
3. **Pass 3 — Verification and Polish** (Lane E validates via browser testing).

---

### 21. Phase Governance Model
* **Entry Gate:** Stable truth, impacted metrics defined, scenarios support the feature.
* **Exit Gate:** Feature matches truth, views aligned, artifacts reviewed, browser walkthrough passes.
